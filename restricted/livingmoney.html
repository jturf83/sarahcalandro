LIVING MONEY, CAPITAL ONE LABS / 2013

Living Money is an experience designed within the checking account to help people know how much they have to spend or save after they’ve covered all their obligations. It does this by identifying peoples’ recurring bills and incomes and then calculating how much will be left of their balance once everything is accounted for. It also summarizes peoples’ monthly income and spend so they can get a quick 10,000 foot view of how they’re doing.

Background & Approach
Capital One 360 business leaders were looking for a way to generate more primary banking relationships by reimagining the 360 checking experience. My team took a human-centered approach to tackle this prompt, leveraging prior research while conducting our own iterative empathy studies.

Top: money drawings from participitory research; Bottom left: speed-dating interview; Bottom middle and right: synthesis of insights from research

Problem & Mission
The problems we discovered were varied and diverse, ranging from confusion around the transfer of money to life changes throwing everything off balance. However, one stood out as the most universally challenging:


People have a lot of financial clutter and it causes them to do work, day in and day out, to know if they’re okay.

Top: video of how this problem manifested itself for one of our research participants; Bottom: Living Money Personas

For some, doing this work comes naturally while most get overwhelmed and struggle to stay on top. This led us to a few important personas who hack their way around this problem in various ways.

For Optimizers, solving this problem is easy. They make custom solutions – usually in the form of spreadsheets – and, in many cases, enjoy the process of keeping everything up-to-date. They don’t need much help.

Strivers look a lot like Optimizers in the way they behave but the key difference is that they don’t do the work because they want to – they do it because they feel they have no choice. The thing is, they don’t have much wiggle room in their financial lives but they value staying on top. At the end of the day (or every few days), they suck it up and do the work – and it’s a big pain.

Cruise Controllers don’t do the work at all. Instead, they set their bills on autopay and build a large buffer in their checking account so their money stays on autopilot. They’d like to have better clarity but wouldn’t dare build a spreadsheet for it.

From all this, we determined that what most people really need is to be more like Cruise Controllers, but with clarity. They need the painful, repetitive work to be done for them, comfort knowing things are working in the background, and that they’ll be alerted when something’s wrong.

The project mission

Solution
Through ideation, prototyping, and testing, we discovered Living Money – a simple, yet powerful idea to help customers clarify their checking account and quickly know if they are okay. We had a strong hunch that this could bring our mission to life, so we started designing an app as the first instantiation of this concept.

Though it seemed simple on the surface, designing Living Money was much harder than we expected. Thinking through all of the possible variations of forecasting one’s cash flow was complicated; even the concept itself was challenging to communicate. With a team of two designers and multiple engineers, it took a great effort to maintain the simplicity of the idea while solving for all the complexities. There were many design sprints, research sessions, and versions of the UI before we finally landed on the design that worked – and it really did work.

Top: UI ideas, research sessions, and synthesis; Bottom: final UI design for MVP

Project Status
In late 2013, we launched the first pilot of Living Money to around 100 internal participants. By January of 2014, we launched the app externally to 3,000 customers in a pilot that ran for about a year.

Through these pilots, we obtained enough directional validation in Living Money’s effectiveness that the enterprise is investing in it for the long-term. Its future vision is to exist as the premier Capital One checking experience and live as a full-service API that can be leveraged across multiple Capital One touchpoints.
